Vocabulary of Sixtus

Account & Account Scope
“Accounts” are simplest thought of as emails but are actually a reference to the user-ID of a tenant which in turn an email is tied to. On occasion, companies may recycle the user-ids between employees.
In context of the Sixtus Scanner, “Account Scope” refers to the number of accounts a particular account may view. Super-users have potential to view as many has an entire tenant, as they are typically the ones doing the GDPR-process/policy work and must access the statistical overview and data-logs to document GDPR-progress.

Area & Domain
“Area” in graphs and report-columns refer to the overarching knowledge-domain which a GDPR-case pertains. “Area” is the first division of GDPR-data into finer-grained groups and will indicate a coarse topic to the user, for instance, "Health" or "Affiliation".

Cases & Entries
The Sixtus Scanner refers to its findings as Cases and Entries. These are observations made by the Scanner's analysis engine and can be viewed in the report-site in depth in what text in each row are highlighted in the Entry-column.

Categories
As term, “Category” points directly to one of the three overarching GDPR-article and their expected inherent behaviours and safety, documentation, and deletion requirements. The Categories may therefore be seen as a direct manifestation of the GDPR articles, which are meant to ease the categorisation of GDPR-data found in a tenant according to its general, supposed treatment.
The three articles are:
    Article 5 (Green, called “GDPR”)
    Article 9 (Red, called “GDPR Sensitive”)
    Article 10 & 11 (Yellow, called “GDPR Confidential”)

Classifications
The “Classification” of GDPR data is a descriptive subsection of Categories paralel to "Area"/"Domain". It is a Classification is description of the legal understanding of the type of information found, for example "Personal Information", "Political Belief", or "National Identification Information".

Confidential Data
Confidential, as per the Data Category, is a type of data specifically pertaining data of legal, financial, or identification nature. It carries the Yellow colour in the Sixtus Scanner and Outlook Tag.

Overview-page
Refers to the graphical front page of the Cloud Scanner Web Application.

Report-page
Refers to the row site with GDPR Entries.

Sensitive Data
Sensitive Data is a type of data pertaining health related or affiliation information (meaning specific types of opinions, memberships or preferences). Such information is dyed red in the Overview, Report site and in Outlook’s tags.

Super-user
A user with more priviledges than the regular user. Typically, these are either IT-specialists or HR-employees or other data/privacy responsibles in either the company’s Cloud or GDPR-processes.

Tenant
A Tenant is a sandbox-type environment in Microsoft Office 365 Data Centre. A tenant is thus a platform of numerous applications, such as mails and cloud-storage/Exchange, domains, web-applications, PowerBI and so on for companies and their employees.

When Sixtus refer to a “subscription” or “Tenant”, we specifically mean the subscription which the company has in the MS 0365 Data Center, which holds all the users of the company. Typically, it would occur once we explain the scope of the scanner in terms of its user-reach; The Sixtus Scanner scans specific items within the company’s tenant, its o365 mail, SharePoint and OneDrive.
