What is and what does the Sixtus Cloud Scanner do?

Sixtus Cloud Scanner is a piece of software installed in a company's MS Azure cloud-platform (what we refer to as the "tenant") similar to other regular applications like Outlook, Word, Excel, etc.

The Scanner is given access to emails and attachments in Outlook and files in OneDrive and SharePoint. The Scanner in turn scans files stored in these applications for personal identifiable information and categorised as per the EU's GDPR directive.

The Scanner finds personal identifiable information in textual, tabulated, and image data and shows its observations in a user-friendly and dynamic way. On the company Scanner web-page, a user may view the data found related to themselves and carry out compliance-related actions.

Data is easily filtered on the Scanner web-page, whether it is by date, sender/receiver or any classification of data, and the web-page also offers seamless deletion of PII-related items.

What can the Scanner do for employees and companies?

The Scanner is a tool enabling employees to practically engage GDPR compliance work. This means that the scanner handles identifying, classifying and logging of any personal identifiable information found within a Tenant and furthermore encourages data-minisation through deletion as well as report-generation for documentational purposes.

Employees are liberated of the very task of finding and identifying sensitive data across a vast data-platform, as well as spared the required knowledge and education to classify said data. Instead, employees can focus on their area of expertise and enjoy handling compliance work through clicks of a few buttons.

Companies may also on a general level draw use of the scanner. While employees collectively work towards individual data minimisation, a company may generate reports of the overall progress to document other compliance progresses such as data- and deletion policies, ISO/ISAE certifications, and so on. These collective tasks also serves as a bulwark against data breaches.
