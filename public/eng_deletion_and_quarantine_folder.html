Quarantine Folder and Email Deletion.

This Section explains the flow and dynamics of deletio of emails and what users are to expect once initiating any type of deletion.

Deletion through Sixtus Scanner

Momentarily following a deletion of an email from the Sixtus Scanner Webapp, the mail-row will disappear from the report-site, while the Overview Page will update its statistics upon the following database-update (once every 5. minute).
The email containing GDPR-data will immediately be transported to the “GDPR Quarantine”-folder created in the mailbox of whomever "owned" the email.

The purpose of the GDPR Quarantine folder is to act as a GDPR-data ONLY folder, so that the GDPR-data deleted within an account is always accessible without the necessity to filter between unwanted or already deleted mail.

Any deletion made inside the cloud scanner is instantaneously (upon refresh + reload) reflected in the statistical oveve rview while the Top-Bar will update once the following ingestion function (Database call) runs. The default of the ingestion is 10 minutes and also requires a Refresh + Reload.



Deletion in Outlook

If a user decides instead to delete tagged emails from their outlook directly, their emails will as usual go to their "Deleted Folder" and not rerouted through their Quarantine Folder.


Furthermore, by default, the scanner does not track items in the deleted folder, be it GDPR-tagged or not. This is to avoid any confusion between web-app deletions and regular outlook-deletions and in continuation as to what is being displayed in the web-app.


It is only the default Deleted Folder which the Scanner ignores; any custom-made folders with deletion-rules similar to that of the trash-can will be included in the scanner's statisitics and report-page.
