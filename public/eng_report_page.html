Report-page.

The report-page is the section of the website where users may view all GDPR Cases found in their Account Scope. Super-users will have more Accounts in their scope as they (likely) are administrators of the GDPR-process within the company. The report-page is where the user is sent once they have filtered in the Overview-Page and may view whatever section of data selected.

The report-page consists of three components:

    a CSV-generation button which allows users to retrieve data shown in the report page.
    a Filter-button to re-do a filtering on data (explained here)
    and selected GDPR-data in rows.

GDPR Data

The GDPR-data appears in row-like entries where each GDPR-Case selected either through the Overview-page or Data-Filter can be viewed in detail by clicking on the "+" on the left side.

The user may also from the Report-page delete entries by clicking the square box. The check-box is dynamic and the user may auto-select several GDPR-Cases/Entries at the time by hold down Shift while clicking.

The column-top check-box auto-selects all GDPR-Cases in the set of Data. By clicking “Yes” on the delete button, the mails are moved from their folder of origin to a newly made "GDPR Quarantine"-folder and the statisical record on the Overview Page is also updated momentarily.



Each row consists of the following columns:

Date: The timestamp which the message or file was received.
Account: Which account said message or file belongs (whose mailbox it was found in, and if SharePoint/OneDrive, whose account create/uploaded the file).
Message / File Information:
Email
From: Who sent the message
To: Who received it (May say “multiple recepients”)
Folder: In what folder the user may find the email
Subject: The subject of the mail
OneDrive & SharePoint
Location: Specification of which storage
Filename
Path: URL to the given path
GDPR Components: holds an overview of the GDPR-Cases found within the mail or file and what components in that entry holds what. There are two forms:
“Message Body”: -> X GDPR Entries
The GDPR-Cases come from an email text-body
Filename -> X GDPR Entries
The GDPR-Cases come from an attachment, OneDrive or Sharepoint
If the GDPR-Component has an exclamation mark, the colour will indicate the nature of the GDPR-Case according to the colouring scheme on the front page. There exists only Red and Yellow marks
