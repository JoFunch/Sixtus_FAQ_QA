Scanning

This page hopes to bring as much transparency to the scanner and its logics and functionality for its users to understand.

The First Scan

Once the scanner is installed, the scanner does by default not initiate any scanning. This default setting prevents any unwanted scanning-initiation and allows the company to control the initiation, scalability, and cloud resources of the scanner.

The Superuser must in their Configuration page allow users to be scanned or skipped before the scanners ingestion (data fetch/loading) function will feed the scanning-queue (what the scanner fetches to scan and inserts into its database). Once users are added, the scanner will begin scanning and not before.

Once the Scanner is initiated, the scanner will follow a "work until finish"-logic. The Scanner will queue mails, attachments, OneDrive, and SharePoint until selected users are depleted. Users may always be added or skipped in the Configuration-Page.

A Regular Scan

Once the scanner is up to date (i.e nothing in its queue), it will enter another "mode" of scanning. At this point, the scanner will go into sleep-mode at 6 PM Central European Time and resume at 8 AM.

Sleep-mode

While the sleepmode will imply large resource-savings, the desire of scanning-completion and the speed and urgency implied expected remains a priority.

Something in queue

As a rule, the scanner's ingestion function continuously feeds a scanner's queue, and in turn, if there is something in queue, the scanner will continue to scan.

While the scanner does have a sleep-mode, the sleepmode is only activated once the scanner is done (i.e. once the queue is empty). By this logic, the scanner will not enter sleep mode when the company scales its users in the scanner, but work until completion.
